Black African Women Entrepreneurs

These female entrepreneurs are breaking barriers, transforming industries and inspiring change on the continent. Starting a business is not for the faint of heart, but that didn't stop these women from doing it.

Portia Mngomezulu

Portia M Skin Solutions

Mongomezulu, registered her business with only one product to offer. She took her business from casual sales to friends, family and Facebook acquaintances, to an entire range now sold in some of the biggest retail stores in Southern Africa.

Ntombenhle Khatwane

Afro Botanics

While working in her government position of eight years, entrepreneurship called Khatwane and she attempted to embark into asphalt manufacturing then into stationery production. After those first two endevors failed, she finally decided to form her own haircare comapny. Today her full range of products is on national retailers' shelves and she's branching out into shampoos and body washes for the hotel industry.

Nontando Molefe

Phatsima Jewellery Designs

Molefe started her own business from a garage at home home in Rndburg when it became apparent that her degree in Jewellery Designing and Manufacturing was just the tip of the iceberg of what she still had to learn.

Sandra Mwiihangele

Kiyomisandz Beauty Products

Cosmetic chmist Sandra Mwiihangele hails from Namibia, and while working at a cosmetic manufacturing laboratory in Cape Town, she acquired the tools needed to launch an advanced cosmetic company with a difference. Today her cosmetic and toiletry company has earned the 2015 National Commission on Research, Science & Technology innovation grant.

Rachel Sibande

mHub

Upon her return from the Young Africa League of Nations Programme in Chicago, USA in 2012, Sibande was inspired to launch Malawi's first tech incubator a year later. Not only is she a computer scientist and recognised technology advocate, she was named one of Africa's 30 most promising entrepreneurs under the age of 30 by Forbes in 2016.

Funke Bucknor-Obruthe

Zapphaire Events

Leaving her job at a legal firm two weeks after starting was a bold move the law graduate made to pursue an industry that no one took seriously in Nigeria. From weddings in Lagos and other locations in Nigeria to destination weddings in Europe and beyond, Zaphaire has earned its stripes thanks to Bucknor-Obruthe's perserverance, creativity and flexibility.
